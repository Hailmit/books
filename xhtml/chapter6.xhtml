Chapter Six MORTIMER

Mortimer had slept for only a few hours, which was not enough sleep for a cat. He blinked at the boy, who was now opening the small library doors.

His library’s first visitor! He summoned his strength and sat up.

The boy was Evan, he remembered. Like his hearing, Mortimer’s memory was excellent. From his many hours of sitting at his upstairs window in the History House, Mortimer had learned a lot about the people of Martinville, including most of their names.

He watched the boy read Al’s sign. TAKE A BOOK, LEAVE A BOOK. OR BOTH.

A face, Mortimer thought, can also be a question mark.

Unexpectedly, he felt his small heart pounding. He wanted this boy with a question-mark face to take a book.

Evan’s hand darted into the library.

Good, Mortimer’s heart said. Good.

As he watched Evan walk away, Mortimer felt his exhaustion return. But the sight of Al, who was now coming across the lawn with his food dish, gave him strength.
